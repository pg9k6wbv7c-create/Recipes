Green Chile Stew

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (pork shoulder, chicken thighs, any beef cut) cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, roasted and chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, roasted and chopped
  • 4 cups green chiles (poblano, anaheim, hatch, serrano, jalapeno), roasted and chopped (mild or hot, based on preference)
  • 4 cups chicken broth (beef broth if using beef)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Roast the green chiles, onion, and garlic. You can do this by placing them under a broiler, on a grill, or directly over a gas flame until the skins are charred. Once roasted, place them in a sealed plastic bag for about 10 minutes to steam. Then peel off the skins, remove seeds if desired, and chop them finely.
  2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the pork cubes and brown them on all sides. Remove the pork and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  4. Add the roasted green chiles to the pot and stir to combine with the onions and garlic.
  5. Return the browned pork to the pot. Pour in the chicken broth and bring the mixture to a boil.
  6. Add the ground cumin and dried oregano. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the pork is tender.
  7. Salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro if desired.
